# Markets and Participants

Tags: #finance 

In finance, primary and secondary markets are two distinct but interconnected parts of the capital market where securities are issued and traded. They serve different purposes in facilitating the flow of capital and providing liquidity.

## Primary and Secondary Markets

In Simple terms,the primary market is the market for "new" securities, and the secondary market is the market for "used" securities.

### Primary Market

The primary market is where **new securities are issued for the first time** by companies, government, or other entities directly to investors to raise capital. Think of it as a "birthplace" of a security.

#### Key Characteristics :  

- **Purpose**: The main purpose of primary market is *capital formation*. It allows issuers to raise fresh funds for various purposes such as expansion, repayment of debt, or for infrastructure projects
- **Participants**: The transaction occurs directly between *issuer*(The company or government selling the security) and the *investor* (the person or institution buying it).
- **Issuance**: Securities are sold directly to the public or investors. Investment banks often acts as intermediaries(underwriters) in the process, helping the issuer price the securities, market them, and facilitate the sale.

#### Common Types of Primary Market Offerings:-

- **Initial Public Offering(IPO)**: When a private company sells its shares to the public first times, becoming a public traded company.
- **Follow-on Public Offering(FPO)**: When a company that is already public traded company, issues additional shares to raise more capital.
- **Rights Issues**: An offer to existing shareholders to buy new shares, usually at discounted price, in proportion to their current holdings.
- **Private Placement**: Securities are sold to selected group of institutional investors (e.g., mutual funds, hedge funds, pension funds) rather than the general public. 
- **Debt Issuance**: When government issues treasury bonds or corporate issues corporate bonds for the first time.

### Secondary Market

The secondary market is where **already-issued securities traded between investors**. The issuing company or government generally not involved in these transactions and does not receive any proceeds from sales in secondary market.
#### Key Characteristics : 

- **Purpose**: The primary purpose of secondary market is to provide **liquidity** to investors to facilitate **price discovery**.
	- **Liquidity**: Investors are more willing to buy securities int the primary market if they know they can easily sell them later in the secondary market.  
	- **Price Discovery**: The continuous buying and selling my millions of investors in the secondary market helps determine the current price of a security based on supply and demand, company performance, and investor sentiment.	
- **Participant**: Transactions occurs between two investors.
- **No Issuer Involvement**: The issuing company does not gain or lose any money when its shares or bonds are traded in secondary market.

- **Types**:-
	- **Exchanges**: Organized marketplace like stock exchange(e.g., NSE (National Stock Exchange) and BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)) in india where securities are traded according to strict rules.
	- **Over-the-Counter(OTC) Markets**: Decentralized market where trading occurs directly between parties (e.g., through a network of dealers) rather than on a formal exchange. 


### Key Markets Players

Key market players include dealers, brokers, financial intermediaries, and you and me. Each of these players facilitate exchange of products, and capital in different ways. Presence of these players makes financial transactions easier, faster and safer - essentially more efficient.

#### Dealers

Financial dealers owns the security that they buy or sell. When a dealer engage in financial transactions, they are trading from their own portfolio. They profit from the **bid-ask spread**, which is the difference between the price they are willing to buy a security(the bid price) and the price they are willing to sell it(the ask price).By standing ready to always buy or sell, dealers increase the efficiency of the market.

#### Brokers

Brokers act as facilitators in a market, and they bring together buyer and seller for a transaction. Brokers differ from dealers who buy and sell from their own portfolios of holdings. These firms and individuals traditionally receive commission on sales. 

In a world of stockbrokers, you may work with discount broker or a full-service worker, and the fees and services are significantly different. A discount broker execute trades for clients. Brokers are required for clients because security exchange needs membership in the exchange to accept orders. 
Full service broker offer more services and charges more fees and commission than discount broker. Full service brokers offer investment advice, retirement planning, and portfolio management, as well as execute transactions. 

#### Financial Intermediaries

A **Financial Intermediaries**, such as a commercial bank, or a mutual fund investment company, serves as intermediary to enable easier and more efficient exchanges amount two transacting parties. For instance, commercial bank accepts deposit from savers and investors and create loan for borrowers. 

Financial institution usually facilitate financial intermediation. However, occasionally landers and borrowers initiate transactions without the help of financial intermediary. When this occurs on the large scale, the process , known as disintermediation, can cause much turmoil in the financial market.

- The advantage of a robust network of financial intermediary are many.
	- They add efficiency to the financial system through lower transaction cost.
	- They gather and disperse financial information to minimize financial abuse and fraud.
	- They provide economics of scale and specialized knowledge.
	- Financial Intermediary are critical for the functioning of a capitalist economy.
