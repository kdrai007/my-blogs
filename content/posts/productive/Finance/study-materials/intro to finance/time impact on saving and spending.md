# Impact of Time on Spending and Saving

The choice to spend or save and invest in really choice between consumption today versus consumption in future. An important aspect of trade-offs between spending and saving involves your short-term and long-term goals. Delaying consumption until later comes with risk.

- Will your consumption choice still be available?
- Will the prices be attainable
- Will you still be able to consume and enjoy your future purchases? 

When saving for **short-term** objectives, the safety of the principal invested is important, and the value of returns is minimal compared to long-term investment.Most short-term investor have low tolerance for risk and hope to beat the rate of inflation with a little extra besides.

- Low risk low return.

An **intermediate** investment may be to save for a new car or for down payment for house or plot. Again, maintaining the principal is important, but you have some time to recover from poor investment returns.  

- intermediate-term investment tends to earn higher average annual rate of return than short-term investments.

Long-term investment have advantage of enough time to recover from temporary poor performance and the luxury of compounded returns over a long period.

- Long term investment, tends to have higher risk and higher expected annual rate of returns.

## Economic Value

Value is a term  used frequently in  business and especially in economics, accounting and finance. Financial people likes to talk in terms of "market value." Market value is calculated on future cash flows discounted to today. Market value is the prices that consumers pay for product. **Economic Value" is what we believe consumers actually willing to pay for a product, service or experience.
