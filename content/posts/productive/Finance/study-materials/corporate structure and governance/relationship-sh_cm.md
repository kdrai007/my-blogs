# Relationship between Shareholders & Company Management.

## Stakeholders

A stakeholder is a person or group that has interest in the outcome of an organization's actions. A stakeholders include employees, shareholders, suppliers, customers, communities, and government.Different stakeholders have different priorities,  company's often have to make compromises to please as many stakeholders as possible. 

### Shareholder's Roles and Composition.

A shareholder or stockholder can be a person, company or organization that holds stock in given company. Shareholders receives dividend if the company is doing well in the market.They are entitled to vote in certain company matters and to be elected as member of board of directors. One advantage to be a shareholder is you are not eligible to bear company's debts.

#### Types of shareholders

There are two types of shareholders: *common shareholders** and **preferred shareholders**. Common shareholders are those who owns common stock of company. They have right to control how the company is managed, and they have right to bring changes  if management is involved in activities that can potentially affect business. 
Preferred shareholders owns shares of company's preferred stock and have no voting rights or involvement in managing the company. Instead they receive fixed annual dividend, apportioned before common shareholder receives their dividends.

- Both common shareholder and preferred shareholders 's stock value changes in case company's doing well.
- Shareholders and directors are two different entities, although director can also be a shareholder. 

### Role of Management.

Corporation are run by a group of senior managers referred to as the board of directors(BOD).The Bod is ultimately responsible for providing oversight and strategic direction as well as overall supervision at the highest managerial level. From an operational standpoint, the practical day-to-day management of company comprises team of several mid-level managers, who are responsible for the leadership of various departments.

- Primary role of any management group involves setting the objective of the company, organizing the company, hiring, leading and motivating employees.  
- It's very important that managers focus more on long-term goals rather then short-term goals.
