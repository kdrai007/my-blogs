# Microeconomics

Microeconomics studies individual economic agents and their decisions & actions,within the economy. The interactions of the decisions that business and customer make will determine the price and quantity of a good or service that is sold in the marketplace. Financial managers need solid foundation in **Microeconomics**. This foundations help them understand the market for the company's products and services including pricing considerations. 


### Demand

A successful company cannot just create or manufacture a product  or provide a service; it must produce a product or service that customer are happy to purchase. **Demand** refers to the quantity of a product or service that consumers are willing to purchase at various prices. 

Let's consider this, what demand for pizzas  looks like, suppose that at a price of 30$ per pizza, no one will purchase that pizza (Unless there's gold hidden in it), but if the price of the pizza is $10 , 40 people might purchase that pizza as show in the table below.

| PIZZA Price | Quantity |
| ----------- | -------- |
| 30$         | 0        |
| 25$         | 10       |
| 20$         | 20       |
| 10$         | 40       |
| 5$          | 50       |

According to the information given in the table, let's plot a graph on the same data.Economists refer to the line shown in the graph called *demand curve*. When plotting a demand curve price is placed at vertical axis, and quantity should place on the horizontal axis. Because more pizzas are bought at lower prices, the demand curve is downward sloping. This inverse relationship is referred to as the **law of demand**

![[Pasted image 20250901222614.png]]

The inverse relationship between the price of goods and the quantity of goods sold occurs for two reasons.First if you consume more and more pizza, the amount of happiness will also diminishes with pizza. If you are hungry you might willing to pay more for pizza, and that pizza will bring great satisfaction to you, however after your hunger is finished, you may not be willing to pay as much for second pizza. Second, demands depends not only on your willingness to buy pizza but also your ability to pay, you simply can not buy as much pizza

If other relevant economic factor changes, the demand curve can change. Relevant economic factor would include consumer income, the size of the population, the taste and preference of consumer, and the price of other goods. For example, If price of hamburger doubled, then consumer would substitute with pizza or any other relevant item.This would increase demand for pizza.

### Equilibrium Price

Demand represent buyers,and supply represent sellers. In the market these two groups interact to determine the price of a good and the quantity of goods that is sold. Because both the demand curve and supply curve are graphed with price as vertical axis and quantity as horizontal axis these two curve can be placed in the same graph, as show down below.

![[Pasted image 20250902213900.png]]

- Graph of Demand and Supply showing Equilibrium price and quantity.

The point at which demand and supply curves intersect is known as **equilibrium**. At the **equilibrium price**, the quantity demanded will equal exactly the quantity supplied.There is no shortage or surplus of the product.
