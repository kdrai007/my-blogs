# Finance

[source](https://openstax.org/books/principles-finance/pages/1-1-what-is-finance)


Finance is the study of management,movement, and raising of money. The 'Finance' can be used as a *verb*, such as First National Bank agrees to finance your home mortgage loan. At its essence, the study of finance is about understanding the uses and sources of cash. 

- It can be also be used as a noun referring to an entire industry.
- Finance can also be a tool to make us better at decision making.

Finance is divided into three primary areas in the domestic market :-

1. Business Finance 
2. Investment Finance
3. Financial markets and Institutions.

### Business Finance

Business finance looks at how managers can apply financial principals to maximize value of the firm in a risky environment. Business have many stakeholders. In the case of corporations, the stakeholders owns and the company and hire managers to run the company with the intent to maximize stakeholders profits.
Consequently, all management decisions should goes from these filters: "How does this decision will impact the profit?", and "Is this the best decision to be made for stakeholders"

In Business Finance, managers focus on three broad areas:-

1. **Working Capital Management** :- WCM is study and management of short-term assets and liabilities. The Chief Financial Officer(CFO) and the financial team are responsible for managing company policy regarding WCM. 

- Financial department role
    - Determines the credit policy
    - Establishes minimum criteria for the extension of credit to clients.
    - Terms on lending, When to extend.
    - When to take advantage of short-term creditor financing

- The accounting department basically implements the finance department's policies. In many cases the accounting team and financial team work along, in others, they work separately.

2. **Capital Budgeting** :- CB is the process of determining which long-term or fixed asset to acquire in effort to maximize shareholders value. 

- Capital Budgeting decisions adds the greatest value to a firm.
- Capital Budgeting is thought to be one of the most important financial function within a firm. 


3. **Capital Structure** :-  CS is the process by which managers focus more specifically on long-term debt and increasing shareholder's wealth.Capital Structure questions require financial managers to work with economists, lenders, underwriters, investment bankers and others sources of external financial information and financial capital. 


### Investment

- **What are investments?**
    - Investments are products and processes used to create and grow wealth. 

- **What we study in investments?**
    - Different types of financial tools ( like stocks and bonds)
    - Different ways to invest (like mutual funds and retirements plans)
    - How to think about risk and return

- **Types of things we can invest in**
    - stocks : Buying a small piece of ownership in a company
    - Bonds :  Lending money to company or government with the promise they'll pay you back with interest.
    - Derivatives : More complex tools like future and options, whose values comes from another asset.

- Investment is very interesting area of finance for students 
- Generally, students assume investing is very glamours or dangerous for them due to how media represents it in movie and programs, but the reality is different compared to those portrayals.

### Financial Markets & Institution 

Financial Markets and institution are the firms and regulatory agencies that oversee our financial systems. There is overlap in this area with investment and business finance, as the firms involved are profit seeking and need good financial management.

- They also are commonly the firms that facilitate investment practices for our economy.
- A financial institution regulated by federal or state agency will likely handle individual investment such as purchase of stocks or mutual funds.

**What lead to creation of Financial markets and Institutions**

US regulatory structure for financial market and institution developed in 1930s. This development was direct response to the *stock market crash in 1929 and subsequent Great Depression*. The driving force for the creation of Financial market and Institution was desire for safety and protection for investors and financial industry.

Key development during the periods included :-

- The formation for the **Securities and Exchange Commission(SEC)** 
 Major bank regulation in the form of **Glass-Steagall Act(1933)** and **Banking act 1935** give rise to government-backed bank deposit insurance and more robust Federal Reserve Bank.

- After the **Great recession in 2007** many regulations were passed to protect the industry, some are *Dodd-Frank act of 2008 for reorganizing and toughening the regulation industry*
- Dodd-Frank also created **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau**(CFPB) for consumers.
- In addition to the post-recession regulations, **Financial Industry Regulation Authority**(FINRA) formed in 2007 

### Risk and Return in Finance

Finance tells us increase in risk also means increase in expected return. The study of Historical financial markets demonstrates that this relationship generally holds true that the riskier the investment over time has provided greater returns.Of course, it's not true all the time, sometime or major of times investors losses their investment in loss.

At its basic level, risk is uncertainty. The study of finance attempts to quantify risk in a way that helps individual and organizations assess an appropriate trade-off for risk.

- Risk-return trade-offs are all around us in everyday decision-making.

Return is a compensation for making an investment and waiting for benefits. Return could be interest earned on an investment in a bond or dividend from the purchase of stock.Return could be also higher income received and the greater job satisfaction realized from investing in a college education. 

- Individual tends to be risk averse. This means that for investors to take greater risks, they must the expectation greater return.
- Investors would not get satisfied if return on bonds and stocks are same as risk-free savings accounts.

### Glossary

1. Derivatives: Derivatives in the stock market are contracts whose values comes from underlying assets, such as stock, bonds, or indices.
2. Financial Markets & Institutions: Agencies that oversee financial systems. 
3. Mortgage Loan: Type of loan used to purchase real estate,where the property itself is for collateral.
4. Dividend: A dividend is a payment made by a corporation to their shareholders.
5. Stocks: Stocks are shares of ownership in a company.
6. Bonds: A bond is type of investment where you lend money to a government or corporations in exchange for periodic interest payment and return of bond's face value.
7. Securities: Securities are essentially tradable financial assets.They represent a specific financial value and can be bought, sold, or traded in financial markets
