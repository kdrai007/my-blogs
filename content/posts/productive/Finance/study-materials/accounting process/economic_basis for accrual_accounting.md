### The Accounting Equation

It can be helpful to think of the **accounting equation** from a "sources and claims" perspective. Under this approach, the assets(items owned by the organizations) were obtained by incurring liabilities or were provided by the owners. Stated differently, every asset has a claim against it - by creditors and/or owners.

`Asset = Liabilities + Owner's equity`

We need to examine several underlying concepts that forms the foundation for the accounting equation: the double-entry accounting system, debits and credits and the 'normal' balance for each account that is part of the formal accounting system.

- The three core components :-

1. Assets
    - Assets are resources that company owns or controls that have economic value. These are things that brings future benefits.
    - Example:  Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Building ,and Land.

2. Liabilities
    - Liabilities are obligations or debts that company owes to outside parties. They represent the future outflow of economic benefits.
    - Example: Accounts Payable(money that company owes to suppliers), Loans Payable, and Deferred Revenue.

3. Equity
    - Equity are owners' or shareholders' residual claims on the assets of the company after all liabilities are paid off. It represents the value financed by the owners. 
    - Example: Common Stock(money invested by owners/shareholders), and Retained Earnings(accumulated profits kept in the business).

### Double-entry accounting 

Accounting is based on double-entry accounting system, which require the following :-  

- Each time we record a transactions,we must record a change in at least two different accounts.Having two or more accounts change will allow us to keep the accounting equation in balance.
- Not only will at least two account will be affected, but there must be at least one debit and one credit side impacted.
- The sum of debit and credit must be equal for each transaction.
