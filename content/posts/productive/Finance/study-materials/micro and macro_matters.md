# Microeconomics and Macroeconomics Matters

Microeconomics and Macroeconomics are two fundamental branches of economy, and their relevance in finance is profound ,albeit at different level of analysis. They provide complementary perspective that are essential for making informed financial decisions, whether you're an individual investor , a corporate CFO, or a policymaker. We typically divide economy in two major parts, Micro and Macro economics 

- Economy is the study of the allocation of scarce resources.
- Economy tries to understand the how and why of human and financial capital allocation to governments, businesses and consumers.

## Microeconomics

Microeconomics is devoted to studies of these decisions of allocation of individual business, persons, or organizations. Microeconomics helps us understand incentives and behaveiour, customer choices and consumption, and supply and demand. 

Our understanding of microeconomics aids in financial forecasting, planning, and budgeting by understanding how individuals are likely to react to changes in product or service functionality ,price, supply, quality, marketing  or other firm-included stimulus. 

#### Microeconomics helps us understand: 

- **Individual Investment Decisions**
    - **Utility Maximization**: How individuals make choices about saving vs spending.
    - **Risk-return Trade-off**: How an individual investor assesses the risk and potential return of a specific stocks, bond or mutual fund based on their personal finance situation.

- **Corporate Finance Decisions**
    - **Capital Budgeting**: How a company evaluates specific investment projects (e.g., building a new factory, developing a new product) by analyzing their cash flows, internal rate of return, and net present value.
    - Capital Structure, Dividend policy, Valuation, Market structure and Competition

- **Pricing of Financial Products** 
    - **Supply and Demand**: How the supply and demand of specific financial instrument (e.g., a particular company's stock, a specific type of loan) influence their prices and yields in individual markets.

## Macroeconomics

Whereas microeconomics studies the decision of individuals, Macroeconomics examines decisions by groups. Macroeconomics areas of study and concern include inflation , income, economy growth, and unemployment.   

Some of the key question addressed by macroeconomics include: What causes unemployment? What causes inflation? What creates or stimulates economic growth? Macroeconomic attempts to measure how well economy is performing, understand what forces drive it, and projects how performance can improve.


#### Macroeconomics helps us understand: 

- **Market Trends and Investor sentiment**
    - **Economic cycle**: The business cycle (expansion, peak, recession, trough) significantly impact overall market performance. Macroeconomics indicators helps investor anticipate these cycles and adjust their portfolio accordingly (e.g., moving to defensive stocks during a recession expectations). 
    - **Inflation**: High inflation erodes the purchasing power and reduces the real return on investments. Financial professionals monitors inflation data to make decisions about asset allocation.
    - **Interest Rates**: Central bank monetary policy(interest rate decisions) directly affects the cost of borrowings for companies and individuals.
    - **Unemployment**: High unemployment rate can signal weak consumer spending and struggling economy, which impacts corporate revenues and profit, and thus investment returns.

- **Government Policy Impact**
    - **Fiscal Policy**: Government spending and taxation policy (e.g., tax cuts, infrastructure spending) can stimulate or cool down the economy, influence corporate profits and investment opportunities.
	- **Monetary Policy**: Central bank actions (e.g., quantitative easing, changes in repo rate) directly affects liquidity in financial system, bond yields, and credit availability, impacting all financial market.

### Relationship between Microeconomics and Macroeconomics

To make financial forecasts, managers needs good information to understand the relationship among several economic variables. Working from small to large, sale forecasts estimate the likely price and quantity of goods sold. In doing so forecaster will consider local, regional, state, national and international economic condition

- Inflation is important macroeconomic variable that influences prices.
- Every quarter, financial information hubs like WSJ(wall street journal) or regulatory bodies, releases estimate about expected and current inflation.
- Financial forecaster pays close attention to current and expected interest rates, as they have fundamental impact on the cost of rising funds and determining the required rate of return on investment.


> How do International macroeconomic factors affect investment decisions for businesses and individuals?

- Foreign investment adds risk and potential return to the decision making process.
