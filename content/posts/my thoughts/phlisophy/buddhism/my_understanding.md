# Buddhism

Here, I will just write my understandings of Buddhism after reading books and blogs

- According to `Asanga`, the great Buddhist philosopher of the 4th century A.C, `sraddha` has three aspects
    1. full and firm conviction that a thing is,
    2. serene joy at good qualities, and
    3. aspiration or wish to achieve an object in view.

## The four noble truth

1. `Dukkha` (suffering)
2. `Samudaya` (Origin of suffering)
3. `Nirodha` (Cure of suffering)
4. `Magga` (Path to the end of suffering)

### Dukkha (Suffering)

The conception of `dukkha` can be viewed from these three aspects:-

1. `dukkha` as ordinary suffering
    - All kinds of suffering in life like  birth, old age, sickness, death , association with unpleasant person and conditions, separation from beloved once and pleasant conditions, not getting what one desire, grief, lamentation, distress - all such forms of physical and mental suffering, which are universally accepted as pain or suffering is included in `dukkha` as ordinary suffering. 

2. `dukkha` as produced by change
    - A happy feeling, a happy condition in life is not permanent, not everlasting. It changes sooner of later. When it changes, when it changes it produced pain, suffering and unhappiness. This vicissitude is included in `dukkha` as suffering produced by change`(viparinama-dukkha)`.

3. `dukkha` as conditioned state
    - According to  buddhist philosophy what we called a 'being' or ,an 'individual' ,or as 'I' is only a combination of ever-changing physical and mental forces or energy.
    - `Karma` reflects on not how we see things or how we perceive them but, how we react.
    - Consciousness depends on matters, perception, and mental formations and it can't exist independently of them.
    - Whatever is  impermanent is `dukkha`

- Never give the impression of gloom or sorrow, but produce the atmosphere of calm and serene joy.
