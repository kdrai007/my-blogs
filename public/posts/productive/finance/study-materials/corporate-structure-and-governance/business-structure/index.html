Business Structure

The Most Common Type of Business Organization

The functions of most executive management teams are very similar the most businesses, and they will not differ in any significant manner based on how they may be structured or organized.However , the legal structure of any company will have substantial impact on its operations, and it therefore deserves significant analysis and discussion

The four most common forms of business organizations are:-

  1. Sole proprietorship
  2. Partnership
  3. Corporations
  4. Hybrid, such as Limited Liability Companies (LLCs).

The vast majority of businesses take form of a proprietorship. However based on the total dollar value of combined sales, more than 80% of all businesses in United States are conducted by a corporation

Sole Proprietorships

A proprietorship is defined as an incorporated company owned by a single person. The process of forming business as a sole proprietorship is rather simple. A business owner merely decides to begin conducting business operations and viola a sole proprietorship is formed.

Advantage of sole proprietorship:-

  • They have basic structure and simple and inexpensive to form.
  • They are subject to relatively few government rules and regulations.
  • Taxation on sole proprietorship is far simpler than any other organizational forms.
  • Controlling responsibility of the firm is not divided in any way. This results in less complicated managerial decisions and improved timelines of necessary corrective decisions.

Disadvantages :-

  • A sole proprietor has unlimited personal liability for any financial obligations and debt, so in the end, they risk more incurring financial loss then the total money they invested in the company’s formation.
  • Sole proprietor often depends solely on owner’s personal savings or loans.
  • Lack of business continuity.
  • Limited growth potential.
  • Limited skills and expertise.

Partnerships

A partnership is a business structure that involves legal arrangements between two or more people who decided to start a business together. Just like sole proprietorship, partnerships are fairly easy to establish. Partnerships offer more advantage over proprietorships. Among them, one or more partner can have more different, or higher level of expertise in business then sole proprietors, which can lead to superior management of business. Furthermore different parter can bring different level of capital to a firm, making the initial process of business formation more smooth and less risky.

Risks:-

  • Potential for conflict and disagreements
  • Lack of continuity
  • Shared profits

Corporations

The most common type of organizational structure for larger business are corporations.A corporation is a legal business entity that is created under the laws of state.This entity operates separately and distinctly from its owners and managers. It is the separation of corporate entity from its owners and managers that limit’s stockholder’s losses to the amount they originally invested in the firm. There are different types of corporations, such as C corporations and S corporations, merits and demerits can vary.

Advantages:

  • Limited Liability
  • Easier to raise capital.
  • Perpetual existence - A corporations ’s existence is not dependent on its owners. It can continue to run even if a shareholders dies, become incapacitated,or sell their shares. This provide stability and longevity.
  • Transferable ownership
  • Enhanced credibility - A corporate structure often gives business more credibility and professionalism in the eyes of customers, suppliers, and partners. This can be particularly be helpful when seeking large contract and partnerships.
  • Tax benefits

Disadvantages:

  • Double taxation(C corporation) - The corporation profits are taxed on corporate level. Then when, remaining profits are shared between shareholders as dividends, they are taxed again for personal income returns.
  • Complex and costly to form - Setting up corporations is more complex process and forming sole proprietorship or partnerships.It involves significant paperwork, filing costs , and legal costs.
  • Extensive record keeping and Reporting
  • Increased security scrutiny - Corporations face higher level of scrutiny from government agencies, especially publicly traded companies, which must comply with Securities and Exchange Commission(SEC) regulations.
  • Less controls for founders
  • Limited privacy

Hybrids: Limited Liability Corporations and Partnerships

Another form of business organizations is Limited Liability Corporations(LLC). This type of business structure become very popular these days.The LLC is essentially hybrid form of business. One other type of business organizations is called Limited Liability Partnerships (LLP), which is quite similar to LLC in structure and use.It is very common to see LLPs used as a organizational form for professional services firm, often in fields such as, accounting , architecture, and law.LLCs are typically used for others type of businesses.

  • Similar to corporation structure, LLCs and LLPs will provide their principals with certain amount of liability protection.
  • Limited ownership liability reduces overall risks borne by investors.
