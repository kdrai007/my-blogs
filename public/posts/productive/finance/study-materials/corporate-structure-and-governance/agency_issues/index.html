Agency Issues: Shareholders and Corporate Boards

Agency issues refers to conflict occurs when Agents(manager) who is entrusted with following interest of the principal(shareholders or owner) of an organization abuses their position to further then personal goals. In the field of corporate finance, agency problems often refers to conflict of interest between management of the company and its shareholders.

  • Irrespective of the profit or non-profit business type agency problems occurs everywhere.
  • Every company has its own set of goals or objectives, but the goals of agents and shareholders can differ.
  • These differences exists because every party have desire to maximize their wealth

As with most problematic issues in business, agency issues can be resolved, but only if the organizations are willing to take appropriate steps of resolve them.

Stockholders vs Management

The core different between stockholders and management is the Stockholders owns the company, while management runs it. This creates a classic “agency problems” where principals(shareholders) interest doesn’t always align with its agents(management).

Management which include CEO and other executives, is tasked with day-to-day business decisions, however their personal goals might diverge from shareholder’s objectives of maximizing long term value. This conflict of interest manifests in several ways:-

  1. Risk Tolerance: Shareholders have diversified portfolio and therefore they are willing to take more risk for high value returns. On the other hand management might be more risk averse, they are not willing to accept higher risk for making profits due to chances of losing job and to protect their reputation.

  2. Time Horizon: Shareholders often focuses on long-term wanting sustainable growth and rising stock price over years, while management might be tempted to focus on short-term growth to meet quarterly objective, secure their bonuses or boost share price temporally.This can lead to affect company’s health in long-term run.

  3. Executive compensation: This is huge area of conflict.Management’s pay, salary,bonuses and stock options they are decided by board of directors, if board members are not external they might try to pay more than required compensations to executives even if company is not doing well.

How to resolve Agency Problem

Ultimately agency problems occurs due to differences of interest in parties like management of company, other stakeholders of the firm, and its ownership and stockholders.When perpetuated, these difference may eventually result in lasting conflict of interests. In order to avoid these problems, it is imperative that they address the underlying problems of these differences. This will ensure that normal business will function properly.

While there is no surefire way to resolve all conflicts of interest and agency problems, some measure can help in mitigate such issues include the following :-

  • Offering incentive to management for strong performances and ethical behaviour.
  • Awarding decision makers with stock packages, commission, and other long term compensation packages to encourage long-term thinking and matching of company’s objectives, and ethical behaviour.
  • Penalizing poor performance, shortsightedness, and unethical behaviour.

Impact of ESG(Environmental Social & Governance) Ratings

These days many publicly traded companies, as well as privately owned, are being evaluated and rated according to Environmental, Social and Governance(ESG) factors. These ratings and evaluations are generally conducted by third-party organizations. As a result investor communities are using these ratings to evaluate which company to invest.
