Role of Board Of Directors(BOD)

A board of directors are group of people who are elected to represent shareholders, and these directors are ultimately responsible for running the company. Every public company is legally required to install a board of directors.Nonprofit organizations or private organizations also establish board of directors even if they legally not required to install a BOD.

  • Board of directors is responsible for protecting shareholder’s interests, establishing management policies, overseeing corporations or organizations, and making decisions about important issues.
  • The BOD acts as a fiduciary for shareholders.
  • The board also tasked number of other activities, including setting company gaols, creating dividend and stock policy, hiring and firing Chief Executive Officer(CEO)

Basic structure of Board Of Directors

The bylaws of company or organization determine the structure,responsibilities, and powers given to board of directors. The bylaws also determines how many board of directors there are, how the members are elected, and how frequently they meet. The board must represent shareholders and management interest, so it is best to include both internal and external members.

There are external and internal directors

  • The internal director is member of board who is involved in daily workings of the company and manages interest of shareholders, employees.
  • The external director represent interest of those who works outside of company.
  • CEO often serves as chairman of company’s board of directors.

Corporate Governance

Corporate Governance is a discipline that focus on how a company conduct its business and various controls that are implemented to ensure proper procedure and ethical behaviour.

The main goal is to make sure a company is run in a way everyone get benefits whoever is involved, not just the top guys.

  • The owners(Shareholders) : They want to see their profits grows
  • The employees : They want to work in fair and ethical environment
  • The customers : They want to be treated honestly and get better product.
  • Community and the government : They want company to follow rules and regulation and be a good neighbour.

The role of audit committee

A Strong independent Audit Committee(AC) is an important part of the corporate governance.The AC is formed by the board of directors as a separately charted subcommittee of the bod. Ac takes responsibility for important financial matters, such as reviewing audit plans and findings, approving external public accountant, and coordinating the efforts of both internal and external financial reviews and audits.

Some important functions of audit committee includes :

  • Confirming the accuracy of the firm’s financial reporting.
  • Verifying that systems of internal controls and risk management are working effectively.
  • Ensuring compliance legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Coordinating the activities and performance of the internal audit functions

Importance of Independence of Board of Directors

An independent BOD composed of individuals who have no material interest in the company other than their directorship. They maintain their independence by only accepting compensation from company for their BOD services. It is considered corporate governance best practice to have independent board of directors members for both public and private held companies.

A board with majority of independent directors can bring expertise and objectivity, which

  • Helps assure ownership that company is running legally , ethically in the best interest of shareholders.
  • Enable board members to advance discussions with no hidden agendas for self-advancement or self-profiting motives.
