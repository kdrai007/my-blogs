Interacting with Investors, Intermediaries and Other Market Participants.

Investor Relations

Within the general field of corporate public relations is a specific subdivision referred to as Investor Relations(IR). IR involves elements of communication, marketing, and finance and is designed to control the flow of information of the management of a public corporations to its investors and stakeholders.

In summary, investor relations functions have responsibilities including :-

  • Coordinating live shareholder meetings and press conference.
  • disseminating financial information to the investment community.
  • Conducting briefings to the financial analyst community
  • Publishing the **quarterly report ** and annual report
  • Addressing any issues that arise as a result of financial disclosure.

Quarterly Earnings Conference Calls

Quarterly earnings conference calls are a key component of investor relations in public traded companies. These are the formal events where a company’s management discusses its financial performance for the preceding quarter. The primary reason to perform this activity to create trust and harmony with investors and financial communities.

A typical conference call follows a predictable structure:-

  • Openings remarks and presentations
  • Questions and Answers session
  • Closing remarks

Investment Meetings and Conferences

Organizing the annual meeting of shareholders, investor roadshows, and investment conferences is no easy task for any corporation, though all of these public facing events are critical for maintaining strong relations with shareholders and investors communities. It is important to reach shareholders and investors at almost person level by crafting and share interest investor story.

Important points:-

  • Share the key messages with investors, key messages includes
    • Clear and concise messages
    • The corporate website
    • Share financial and annual reports
  • A productive investor and shareholders meetings begins with a strong understandable corporate introduction follows with engaging story that demonstrate the company’s success.
  • Using senior management’s time efficiently is also important to investor relations.
  • End the meeting with feedback from the investor audience and set-timeline for follow-ups.
