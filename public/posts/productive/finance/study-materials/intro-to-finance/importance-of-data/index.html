Importance of Data

Financial data is important for internal and external analysis of business firms.More accurate and timely data leads to better business and financial decision making. Financial budgeting & forecasting relies on the creation of several types of financial statements(cash flow statement, income statement and balance sheet) as well as notes and assumptions used to create financial statements.

  • Insiders such as executives and middle managers use financial data to evaluate and reevaluate decision-making.
  • Having current and accurate data is key to making consistent value-adding decision to the firm.
  • “Gut feeling” or “Seat-of-the-pants” decision making could inconsistent and disastrous to the firm.

Outsiders also use publicly available data about firms to make purchasing, investment, credit, and regulatory decisions. Consumers,investors, suppliers, lenders and regulators must be able to access firm’s financial information.

Basic Data Types

Financial statements provide some of the data needed for decision making. Firms summarizes data and prepare at least three types of essential financial statement or reports.

  1. The income statement summarizes the flow of revenues and expenses over a specified period.
  2. Statement of cash flow identify actual receipt and use of cash over a period.
  3. Balance sheet shows the existing assets, liabilities, and equity as a particular date.

These statements represents books values and reflect historical costs and accounting adjustments such as accumulated depreciation. Book values often significantly differ from market values. Market values look forward and reflect expectations, whereas book value reflect what has occurred in market.

In addition to internal data summarized on financial statements, firms and outside stakeholders also seek information from outside sources. External data gathering include surveys of customer and suppliers, market research, new product development, agreements with suppliers etc.

The Impact of Digitization

Data digitization makes the storage and transmission of data more cheap and more easy. Digitization transforms nearly every aspect of how financial service are delivered, consumed and regulated. It has fundamentally reshaped the industry, moving from traditionally paper-based, branch-centric model to a highly interconnected, data-driven, and often personalized ecosystem.

Key impacts:-

  1. **Enhanced Customer Experience and Accessibility:-

    • Convenience : Customer can now manage their finance 24/7 from anywhere in the world using apps, and online platforms like, amazon, flipkart etc.
    • Personalization : Leveraging big data and AI, financial institution can offer highly personalized product, services, and advice tailored to individual needs financial advice.

  2. Increased Efficiency and Cost Reduction

    • Automation : Robotic Process Automation (RPA) and AI automate repetitive task like data entry, reconciliation, and compliance check, reducing manual errors operational costs.
    • Improved data management : Digital system allows more efficient storage,collection, transmission of data, and analysis of vast amounts of financial data.

  3. Innovation of New Business Models (Fin-Tech)

    • Digitalization spurred the rise of “Fin-Tech” companies, which are technology-driven startups challenging traditional financial institution with innovative solution.
    • New Products and Services
      • Mobile Payments
      • Robo-Advisors
      • Peer-to-Peer(p2p)
      • Blockchain and cryptocurrencies
      • Reg-Tech(Regulatory Technology)

  4. Data Driven Insights and Analytics

    • Better Forecasting : Access of vast amount of real-time and historical data enables more accurate and up-to mark financial forecasting and predictive analysis.
    • Personalized Marketing : Financial institution can analyse customer behaviour and offer highly targeted product and services.
    • Market Analysis : Sophisticated data analytics tools provide deeper insights into market trends,helping institution and investors make more informed decisions.

Uses of Data

Taking together and separately, internally generated financial statements can provide mangers with wealth of information to enable superior decision making.

Here are six ways managers can use financial statements :-

  1. Measuring the impact of business decision such as new software ,marketing plan or product line
  2. Aiding in development of budgets by creating starting point for future expectations.
  3. Aiding in cost cutting, and duplicate activities.
  4. Providing data-supported strategic planning and visioning
  5. Ensuring consistent data and content across departments.
  6. Motivating teams to set, meet, and exceed goal and objectives.

Glossary

  1. Accumulated Depreciation: Accumulated Depreciation is total amount of depreciation expenses that has been allocated to an asset since it was put into use.
