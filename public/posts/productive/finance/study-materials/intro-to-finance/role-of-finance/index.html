Role of Finance in an organization

Tags: #finance

The Finance Function

Finance have many functionalities within an organization, and there are many job titles to reflect the varied job responsibilities. The Comptroller or controller, in a profit business relies heavily. Controllers are in charge of financial reporting and the oversight of accounting is necessary to develop those reports.

  • Controllers are concerned with payroll functions, accounts receivable, and account payable including taxes, inventory control, and any number of short-term asset and liabilities tracking and monitoring activities.

In most organizations, the treasurer might assume many of the duties of the controller. However, treasurer and also responsible for monitoring cash flow at a firm and frequently is the contact person for bankers, underwriters, and other outside sources of financing.

  • A treasurer may be responsible for structuring loan and debt obligations and determining when and from whom to borrow funds.
  • When a controller faces inward the treasurer faces outward and work as a representative to the public.

The vice president of finance(VP-F) is an executive level position and oversees the activities of comptroller and treasurer. The chief responsibility of the officer is to create and mentor sufficient and qualified staffs that generates reports that are timely, accurate and through.

The chief financial officer (CFO) is in a “big picture” position. The CFO sets policy for working capital management, determines optimal capital structure for the firm, and makes and final decision matter related capital budgeting

  • The CFO is forward looking and responsible for strategic financial planing and setting financial goals.

Financial Planning

Financial planning is critical to any organization, large or small, private or public, for or not-for-profit. Financial planning allows a firm to understand past, current and future funding needs and distribution required to satisfy all interested parties.

  • For-profit businesses work to maximize profit of the owners.
    • These could be shareholders in a public traded corporation.
    • The owner manager of “mom and pop” store.
    • Parters in a law firm.
    • Principal owners of any other number of business entities.

Good financial planning has a number of common features

  • It uses past,current, and pro forma (forward looking) income statement.

  • Cash flow statement are a critical part of any financial planning. Cash flow statement estimate the timing and magnitude of actual cash flows meets financial obligations.

  • Balance sheet are critical for demonstrating the sources and uses of funds for a firm.

  • Forecasting is a form of expected sales, cost of funds, and microeconomics conditions are essential element for financial planning.

Forecasting and Budgeting

Forecasting and Budgeting are common practices in business,governmental agencies, non-profit organizations, and individual households.These activities are essential for businesses and individual alike. Budgeting, or planning for the amount ,sources, uses of cash , occurs early in the planning process. It is common for businesses to developed their an annual budget well before start of year.With budgeting, a firm establishes objectives for upcoming period by developing financial statement based on historical data and expectations, as well as aspirations for future.

  • However, no matter how well the budgeting process goes, actual event can change the timing and magnitude of expected cash flows.

Financial Forecasting addresses the changes necessary to the budgeting process. Budgeting can help identify the differences, or variance from expectations, and forecasting becomes the process for adapting to those changes.The budgeting or planning process is a road map of organizations, and forecasting helps navigate the inevitable detours the firm’s objectives.

Key Role of Budgeting in Finance :-

  1. Setting Financial Goal and Targets
  • Clarity of objective: Budget forces you to figure what you want to achieve financially(e.g., saving money for down payment, for making an investment, advertisement).
  • Quantification: They translate strategic goal in concrete financial numbers, providing measurable targets.
  1. Resource allocation and Prioritization
  • Strategic Spending: Budget helps allocate limited resources to the most important areas, ensuring that spending aligns with strategic priority.
  • Cost Control: They set spending limits on various categories, preventing overspending and identify areas for reduction of cost.
  1. Cash Flow Management
  • Liquidity: Budget helps anticipate periods of cash surplus or deficit, ensuring there’s enough cash to hand to meet those obligations(payroll,bills,debt repayment)
  • Working Capital: They are vital for managing capital efficiently.
  1. Communication and Coordination
  • Alignment: Budgets communicate financial goal across the organization, ensuring everyone working on common financial goal.
  • Stakeholder Confidence: A well-structured budget demonstrates financial prudence and can instill confidence in investor, and other stakeholders.

Key Role of Forecasting in Finance :-

  1. Anticipating Future Financial Conditions
  • Proactive planning: Forecast helps anticipate potential challenges(e.g., dip in sales, rising costs) or opportunities(e.g, market growth) before they occur.
  • Scenario planning: They allow for the creation of various scenarios(best case, worst cast) to prepare for different outcomes.
  1. Adjusting and Adapting plans
  • Flexibility: Unlike static budget, forecasts are dynamic and updated frequently (monthly,quarterly) to reflect changes in market conditions, operational realities and external factors(e.g., economic shifts, new regulations)
  • Course Correction: If a forecasts indicates that budget gaols are unlikely to be met, it prompts management to take corrective actions or revise strategies.
  1. Risk Management
  • Identify Risks: Forecasts help identify potential financial risks (e.g., liquidity shortage,overspending) and develop contingency plan to mitigate them.
  • Evaluating Impact: They allow for the assessment of how various internal and external factors might impact financial performance.

The Relationship: Budgeting and Forecasting Together

Budgeting and Forecasting are intertwined and interdependent.

  • The budget sets the initial targets and direction.
  • The forecast tracks progress against those targets and provides real-time insights into whether those targets are achievable given evolving conditions.
  • Forecasts often leads to revisions or reforecasting of budgets, especially in dynamic environments.
  • Budgeting provides discipline and structure, while forecasts provides the agility and adaptability.

Glossary

  1. Account receivable: Money Owed to You.
  2. Account payable: Money Owed by the business.
  3. Income statement: An income statement summarizes a company’s revenues, expanses, and profit over a specific period
  4. Cash surplus: A cash surplus refer to the amount of cash a company has that exceeds the amount of short-term liabilities.
