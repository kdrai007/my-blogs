Foreign Exchange Rates

The foreign exchange rates is the rate at which one currency can be exchanged with other country one currency. It is the key variable in international and investment.

Spot Exchange Rate

An exchange rate is simply price of a currency. If you live in India and going for trip to USA, you will need dollar, to pay for your hotel, traveling, food and other items.You will need to purchase dollar.Suppose, you need 500 dollar before and your banks tells you need,so,if 1 dollar = 80 rupees then you need 40000 Rs to purchase 500 dollar.

1 USD = 80 INR

USD is an abbreviation of the US dollar and INR is for Indian rupees.This price is known as currency exchange rate, because this is the price you would pay to purchase dollar currency, it is known as spot exchange rate.

Currency Appreciation

Currency appreciation occurs when it costs more to purchase currency then before. Suppose, you go to bank to purchase US dollar and their current price is changed from 1 dollar = 80 rupee to 1 dollar = 85. Hence, the price of dollar has been risen, or the dollar has appreciated.

Currency prices are determined in marketplace through the same types of demand-supply forces. What would cause the dollar to appreciated? Either increase in demand for the dollar or a decrease in supplies of dollars.

Currency Depreciation

Just as currency can be appreciated they can depreciated as well. When US dollar costs less then before it means, its value are depreciated. In case of currency depreciation, either decrease in demand for the dollar or increase in supply for the dollars.

Exchange Rate Risk

Businesses that engages in international business might face currency exchange rate risk. As exchange rates changes, a business can be impacted in number of ways.One of these risk is, transaction exposure, is the risk that expected value of the receipts and expanses will change as a result of a change in currency exchange rate.
