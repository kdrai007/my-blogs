Stages of the Business Cycle

A full business cycle is compromised of two major phases: an expansion and a contraction, with two turning points a peak and a trough.

  • Expansion: This phase is characterized by an increase in output, employment and income. It is a period of general economic growth, with rising business profits and consumer confidence.
  • Peak: The peak represent highest point of economic activity in the cycle. At this stage, growth is at its maximum
  • Contraction: Following the peak, the economy starts to decline. This phase is marked by failing GDP, rising unemployment, and a decrease in consumer spending and business investment. Contraction is referred as Recession.
  • Trough: The trough is the lowest point of economic decline. At this juncture economic activity has bottomed out, and a new expansionary phase is poised to begin
